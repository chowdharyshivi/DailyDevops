HTTPS (Hypertext Transfer Protocol Secure) is a protocol for securely transmitting data 
over the World Wide Web. It is used to encrypt data exchanged between a web server and a client,
 such as a web browser. HTTPS is designed to protect sensitive information, such as login credentials, 
 credit card details, and other personal information, from being intercepted
 and viewed by unauthorized parties.
